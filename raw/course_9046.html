Remark
Teaching goal This course is primarily designed for students in the humanities and social sciences, with supplementary content suitable for students from information and engineering disciplines. The course focuses on issues related to social wealth distribution, incorporating hands-on practice in data mining and visualization using datasets from domains such as economics and internal affairs. Through the application of various software tools, the course aims to enhance students’ practical skills in data analysis and digital tool usage. Learning Objectives: 1. To help students understand the types and causes of contemporary poverty, and to deepen their awareness and concern for issues related to social wealth distribution; 2. To cultivate students’ ability to engage in issue-based inquiry using data-driven evidence, and to improve their familiarity with open public databases; 3. To strengthen students’ competence in applying data software tools, enabling them to analyze texts and datasets, and to develop the analytical skills needed for social issue research; 4. To promote interdisciplinary learning and collaboration among students.

Week 2: Introduction to Data Analysis, Big Data, Data Warehousing, and Introduction to Power BI

Week 5: Teachers' Day (No class)
Week 6: Mid-Autumn Festival (No Class)
Week 7: Poverty and Gender; Introduction to Ministry of the Interior Databases; Data Cleaning with Power Query
Week 8: Poverty and Labor (Class); Introduction to Salary Statistics Databases; Exploring Relational Models
Week 9: Poverty and Urban-Rural Development; Ministry of the Interior Statistical Databases
Week 10: Data Model Building
Week 11: Thematic Data Visualization I: Infographics and Visual Design Elements *(Industry mentor)
Week 12: Thematic Data Visualization II *(Industry mentor)
Week 13: Midterm Presentations
Week 14: group counseling *(Industry mentor)
Week 15: Cloud Platforms and Sharing: From Power BI Desktop to the Cloud, Data Analysis Computation: DAX Functions
Week 16: Final Project Presentations
