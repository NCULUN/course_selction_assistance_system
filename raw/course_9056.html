Remark
Teaching goal

Utilizing the Interdisciplinary AI Credit Program in the General Education Center

Through the utilization of knowledge gained from the courses offered by the Interdisciplinary AI Credit Program in the General Education Center, students collaborate in small teams, employing a division of labor, to create their final projects. This allows students to apply their programming language skills to practical and real-world project applications. From topic selection to data collection, model development, presentation delivery, and written reports, each step presents its own difficulties and challenges, testing students‘ problem-solving abilities.

Qualities that will yield higher scores

  • Creative thinking and problem exploration
  • Efforts to independently think and efficiently solve problems
  • Altruism and a commitment to the greater good

Execution steps

  1. Form AI teams and engage in small group discussions to brainstorm ideas for addressing specific business pain points or social issues. Propose AI-based solutions and conduct conceptual validation.
  2. Assign roles within the AI teams, such as data scientists, data engineers, data analysts, and project managers.
  3. Produce the following deliverables: comprehensive discourse, AI models or algorithms, data collection and analysis processes, human-computer interaction interfaces, project websites, and project presentations.
  4. Encourage participation in suitable AI competitions.

[Course Content]
The "Artificial Intelligence Interdisciplinary Applications Project" focuses on interdisciplinary integration, adopting a Problem-Oriented Project Learning approach. Students are expected to apply relevant mathematics and programming knowledge to identify corporate or societal pain points of interest, propose solutions, and carry out implementation and validation.

[Course Requirements]

Midterm presentation in the 8th session (40% of the final grade), final presentation in the 16th session (40% of the final grade), each group will present for 20 minutes.

[Enrollment Prerequisites]
Students must have completed courses in AI Programming Language, Fundamentals of AI, Interdisciplinary Applications of AI, and Social Applications of AI within their respective college tracks before enrolling in this AI Project Practice course.

[Group Formation]

Groups will be formed in the first class session under the instructor’s guidance based on different functional roles (Data Scientist, Data Engineer, Product Manager). Group brainstorming will follow to develop project topics.

Each group is recommended to consist of 3–5 members.
