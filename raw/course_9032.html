Remark
Teaching goal The world today is vastly different from what it once was. The old saying, "A world apart, a world apart, yet close enough to us," depicts an idealized image of the world. In the 21st century, the international community is developing rapidly. Thanks to technology, we are constantly updated on major global events. As members of the international community, it is essential to understand the logic and major events that govern it, as well as the general framework of international relations. This course, as a general education course, will introduce the workings of international society and politics through a combination of theory and practice. The course is divided into five parts. The first part introduces the basic theories of international relations, including realism and idealism, and the levels of international relations research and analysis, including the international system, domestic, and individual levels. The second part discusses Taiwan's international status, its situation, the interaction between the United States and China, and the diplomatic strategies it should adopt. The third part explains the significance, need, and types of international peacekeeping operations (PKOs). The fourth part explores the issues of international cooperation, international organizations, and globalization, and introduces key international organizations, including the International Monetary Fund, the World Trade Organization, the World Bank, and the European Union. The fifth part explores the issue of globalization, including its definition, formation, and current difficulties. It also explains the causes of the current international financial tsunami and its impact on the current global landscape. The content includes the promise of globalization, the causes and subsequent developments of the financial tsunami, the loss of globalization, the new global landscape, and Taiwan's development.
